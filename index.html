❓ Quick Start Guide Caveman Energy · Sales Engine

What this is: A self-contained sales engine that does three jobs — shows live package prices to discuss with customers, runs a P&L calc to spot-check job profitability, and stores all the underlying material costs in one editable table. Pin to phone, tablet or desktop.

1. Top Toggle Bar

GST treatment (important): All Lookup costs are entered ex-GST (matches what suppliers list net of GST). The P&L Sale Price input is inc-GST — calc strips GST automatically (÷ 1.10). STC rebate handling is owned by Mitch's BAS process; the calculator's gross profit is from his perspective (sales contractor's commission is separate at 7% + GST on the customer-paid amount).

Six buttons at the top control which sections show:

  • 📦 Price Map — customer-facing package grid (default ON)
  • 📊 P&L Calc — internal job profitability (default ON)
  • 🌞 Solar Grid — panel-by-panel pricing rules that drive solar prices on Price Map cards (default OFF)
  • 💲 Price Table — editable cost & sell prices (default OFF)
  • 🔩 Materials — BoM multipliers, trade pricing, site adjustments (default OFF)
  • ❓ Quick Start — this guide (default OFF)

A button greyed/struck-through means hidden. Click to bring back.

2. Package Price Map — Daily Driver

Generates a grid of priced packages with crew labour, materials, STC rebate, and rounding baked in. Two control groups — tap to expand on mobile.

Solar Grid drives most solar prices on cards (see Section 3) — solar-only, add-panels Std, packaged panels, and the 3-month lock-in solar add-on on battery cards. Change a grid rule and those cards repaint live. Combo cards are the exception — they use the engine combo target ★6.0 directly so the combined battery+solar price lands on margin, instead of stacking standalone margins. Battery prices flow from the engine target-★ throughout.

The three table sections:

  • Standalone — dedicated solar visit, costed as addsolar (panels + racking only, no inverter, no new mount kit — we're adding panels to an existing system). Columns: RRP → Cost → PPP → Cash → NP → PPH. Cost includes panel materials, solMat, racking, full-visit labour (7.6 hr floor), overhead, other-wages and 7% commission. With no-floor OFF, small panel counts show inflated Cost (full half-day absorbed by tiny job).
  • Combo — solar bundled with a battery install. Columns: Price → Cost → PPP → +Fin → Save → NP → PPH. Cost is lower than standalone because overhead/wages allocate proportionally by hrs/anchor/crews — not a full day each. +Fin = Price × 1.25 (the 0% finance equivalent).
  • EB — same physical install as combo, $500 off. Cost is identical to combo row-for-row; only the customer-facing Price (and therefore NP and PPH) drops.

Cost derivation: materials (panels + solar mat + racking) + labour (crew × hrs × rate) + overhead share (hrs ÷ 7.6 ÷ crews × overhead rate) + other-wages share (same allocation) + 7% commission on price. Bumping "Crews Running Today" from 1 → 2 drops combo/EB Cost because overhead spreads across more parallel jobs.

Reading PPH: price ÷ crew hours = profit-per-hour proxy. Standalone PPH at low panel counts is artificially low when the 7.6-hr floor is on; flip no-floor mode for the true tag-on PPH on small jobs.

"Add STC to revenue" toggle: Engine NP always includes STC (it's inside the gross profit calc — gpActual = saleEx + stcTotal − costs). When this toggle is ON (default), table NP/PPH show the engine NP as-is, and the STC column is visible. When OFF, the table subtracts STC from displayed NP/PPH so you can see the without-rebate profit, and the STC column hides.

7. How the Numbers Work

The mechanics behind why prices land where they do. Useful when a customer asks "but why?" or when you need to defend a number.

⚙ Day anchor & full-day rounding

A "day" = PLC_ANCHOR (default 12 hrs). Below 7.6 hrs = ordinary rate. 7.6–9.6 hrs = OT @ 1.5×. 9.6–12 hrs = OT @ 2.0×. Past 12 hrs the day resets and the next day starts at ordinary again. Travel adds 1 hr per day on site (so a 2-day job adds 2 travel hrs). Day count for fixed costs: ceil(workHrs / 12). A 13-hr job = 2 days for travel + fixed cost purposes.

⚙ Hour floors (why tiny jobs aren't tiny prices)

  • Standard solar / addsolar floors at PKG_FLOOR_STD = 7.6 hrs — a 3-panel job still books a half-day minimum.
  • Special same-day addsolar floors at PKG_FLOOR_SPC = 1.0 hr — bundled while we're already on site, no separate trip.
  • Special target rating = PKG_TARGET_SPC ★0.5 instead of the standard ★. We accept thinner margins because we're already there.

⚙ Per-panel labour scaling

Solar labour = panelQty × (PLC_HRS_STRING / 7). Default 1.3 hrs per 7-panel string = ~0.186 hrs/panel. So 3 panels = 0.56 hrs labour, 14 panels = 2.6 hrs, 28 panels = 5.2 hrs. Then the floor kicks in for small jobs.

⚙ Even-step pricing (battery boundary)

When ON, batteries step in even increments past pure cost-based pricing:

  • Boundary kWh = 32 kWh by default
  • Below boundary: +$1,500 per +8 kWh module
  • At/above boundary: +$2,500 per +8 kWh module

Why: past 32 kWh you're typically into a parallel stack or 2-day install; cabling/labour complexity steps. All three values are editable in Advanced.

⚙ STC solar rebate

Formula: solKw × zone × deemingYears × $/STC. Defaults: Zone 3 (SEQ) = 1.382, deeming = 5 years (2026), price = $40/STC. Net effect: ~$276/kW solar rebate. Drops to 4 years from 2027 and so on. Editable per quote in the P&L Calc.

⚙ Battery rebate (CER post-May 2026 tapering)

  • First 14 kWh: full rate (100%)
  • 15–28 kWh: 60% of full rate
  • 29–50 kWh: 15% of full rate
  • Above 50 kWh: no rebate

This is why the battery rebate per kWh shrinks as systems get bigger — it's tapered, not flat. Driven by the CER battery scheme rules.

⚙ Wage rates & on-cost multiplier

Crew base hourly: Tradie + TA + Apprentice at default rates ($65 + $30 + $25 = $120/hr) for a 3-person crew. Each role can be set to one of three (TA / Tradie) or four (Apprentice) experience levels with editable rates. Qty (×N) next to each role multiplies the rate — so 2 tradies on a job = $65 × 2 = $130/hr tradie cost. Total base × PLC_MULTIPLIER covers super, leave, workcover. Apprentice rates default $25 / $30 / $35 / $40 per year (Y1 to Y4). TA and Tradie rates default the same across levels — companies set their actual values.

Multiplier formula:

  • Paid hours/yr = 38 × 52 = 1,976
  • Non-productive = AL weeks × 38 + sick days × 7.6
  • Productive = paid − non-productive
  • Leave factor = paid ÷ productive (typically ~1.13, or +13%)
  • Final multiplier = leave factor × (1 + super% + workcover%)

Defaults: 4 wks AL, 10 sick days, 12% super, 5% workcover → multiplier ≈ 1.32. So $120/hr base × 1.32 = ~$158/hr fully-loaded for ordinary hours. OT 1.5× and 2.0× are NOT multiplied — they're paid as straight loadings on the base $120, since super/leave shouldn't compound on premium rates. Editable per quote in the wage breakdown panel.

⚙ Fixed costs (overheads + other wages)

Two annualised buckets, both default $800/day:

  • Overheads — rent, vehicles, insurance, software, admin (annual ÷ working days)
  • Other wages — owner/office staff salary (anyone not on the install crew)

These are allocated per job, not per labour hour. Allocation depends on job type:

  • Battery-only = 0.5 days (half a day, regardless of stack size up to inverter limit)
  • Solar-only = 0.5 days + 0.25 per extra string past the first. So 6.6kW = 0.5d, 13.3kW = 0.75d, 19.8kW = 1.0d
  • Combo (solar + battery) = totalHrs ÷ 12 (proportional to actual day count)
  • Add-panels (small-job) = scaled by hours/anchor — bigger small jobs absorb more overhead
  • Add-panels (special, same-day) = $0 fixed costs (covered by the parent job we're already there for)

Then divided by crew count — 2 crews halves the calendar-day allocation since both buckets spread over fewer days. So: a 6-hour battery install costs the same in fixed costs as a 4-hour battery install (both = 0.5 day allocation × $800 × 2 buckets = ~$800 fixed). It's job-type based, not hour-based.

⚙ Labour vs fixed costs — what scales with what

  • Labour scales with hours worked (and OT thresholds within each day)
  • Fixed costs scale with day allocation (job-type rules above)
  • Materials scale with system size ($/kW or per-module)
  • Travel scales with day count (1 hr/day at full crew rate)

This is why a 2-day install isn't just "double the 1-day price" — labour doubles, travel doubles, fixed costs double, but materials don't. Adding a battery module costs the per-module material + the labour hours, but doesn't push fixed costs (until the day count rolls over).

⚙ Crew count multiplier

2 crews = same labour hours but elapsed days halve. Used for fixed-cost allocation (overheads, other wages spread over fewer calendar days), not for total hours billed. So a 14-hr job with 2 crews = 1 calendar day @ 14 effective person-hours, but fixed costs apportion as 1 day instead of 2.

⚙ Materials per kW / per job

  • Solar materials: $80/kW (cabling, isolators, conduit)
  • Racking: $60/kW
  • Battery materials: $500 flat per job (regardless of stack size)
  • Mount kit: $252 for new installs only — skipped on addsolar (existing kit reused)
  • Panel labour: 1.5 hrs per 3.3 kW string (7 panels @ 475 W)
  • Battery labour: 1 hr base + 0.5 hr per module

⚙ Round-to per job type

  • Combo / battery-only: $500 (clean customer-facing prices)
  • Solar-only / addsolar: $100 (small jobs would distort with $500 rounding)

⚙ PPH / ★ rating

PPH = net profit ÷ total crew hours. ★ rating = PPH ÷ 100. So ★4.5 = $450/hr profit per crew hour. Default targets: battery ★4.3, combo ★6.0, solar-only ★2.5, addsolar ★1.5. Combo targets higher because the customer is buying both products and overheads spread further.

⚙ Lock-in maths (3-mo solar top-up)

Differential = combo @ ★6.2 − batt-only @ ★4.3. Lock target ★6.2 is slightly above the standard combo ★6.0 to cover the return trip cost (we're sending a crew back later). Customer signs today for the battery, has 3 months to add solar at the locked-in differential. GW + Sigen only — Sungrow excluded.

⚙ Annualised energy & payback

Solar annual = solKw × 1,460 kWh/kW/yr (SEQ irradiance assumption). Battery annual cycles = battKwh × 365. Bill-offset estimates use these to reverse-engineer "years to payback" displayed in the P&L Calc summary. Adjust the kWh/kW/yr figure if quoting outside SEQ.

8. P&L Calculator — Deep Dive

For oddball quotes that don't fit the package map, or to verify what's behind a Price Map number. Every cost line and rate is editable. Also lets you toggle whole cost categories on/off for what-if scenarios.

⚙ Top configuration row

  • Quoting — Combo / Battery / Solar / Add-panels. Drives floors and target ★.
  • Crews — 1 or 2. Halves elapsed days and fixed-cost allocation when 2.
  • STC Period — May–Dec '26, Jan–Apr '27, etc. Auto-sets deeming years on selection.
  • Phase — SP / TP. Drives RCBO type and Sungrow filtering.

⚙ System Design quick-build

Three dropdowns: Inverter, Battery, Gateway/Kit. Picking these auto-fills the line items below with correct quantities and prices. Faster than typing each line item manually.

⚙ Line items table

Each row: Qty, Cost Price, Total. Both Qty and Cost are editable per quote — useful for one-off pricing without changing the global Price Table. Lines covered: Panels, Inverter, Battery, Mount Kit, Gateway, RCBO SP, RCBO TP, EV, Sigen Parts, Switchboard upgrade, DC cable (m), AC cable (m), Trench (m).

Balance hrs dropdown — extra labour hours for site-specific quirks (long cable runs, complex switchboard, second-storey, etc). Adds to total labour without changing materials.

⚙ Cost-line ON/OFF toggles (the powerful what-if switches)

Each cost category has a checkbox to remove it from the calc. Useful for: "what if I absorb this cost", "what if I quote this as a stripped-back special", or "show me labour-only profit".

  • Min hrs floor — disable to let the calc go below 7.6h on tiny jobs
  • STC Rebate — turn off to see price without solar rebate (e.g. quoting cash deals)
  • Battery Rebate — same, for battery scheme
  • Fixed Costs — turn off overheads + other wages. Shows raw labour+materials profit only
  • Labour — strip labour cost (rare — used for material-only break-down)
  • Sale Comms — turn off the commission payout
  • Solar Materials (auto) — disable the $80/kW auto add
  • Racking (auto) — disable the $60/kW auto add
  • Battery Materials (auto) — disable the $500 flat add

All ON by default. Turning anything off here doesn't affect the Price Map cards — only the P&L Calc view.

⚙ STC inputs (per quote, override defaults)

  • STC Period — picks deeming years automatically (5 for 2026, 4 for 2027, etc)
  • Solar deeming yrs — manual override if the period dropdown isn't quite right
  • STC $/STC — default $40. Market price; check before big quotes
  • Battery STC factor — default 6.8 certificates per kWh of battery
  • Battery STC $/STC — default $40
  • STC Zone — default 1.382 (Zone 3 SEQ). Change if quoting outside SEQ — Zone 1 = 1.622, Zone 2 = 1.536, Zone 4 = 1.185

⚙ Wage rate inputs (the breakdown panel)

  • Tradie / TA / Apprentice — individual hourly rates ($65 / $30 / $25 default)
  • Super % — default 12%. Bump to 12.5%, 13% etc as super guarantee rises
  • Workcover % — default 3%. State-specific
  • Annual leave (wks) — default 4 wks
  • Sick leave (days) — default 10 days
  • Productive hrs (display only) — auto-calculated from leave inputs
  • Leave-up (display only) — % cost uplift from non-productive hours
  • Multiplier (display only) — final on-cost multiplier (~1.32 default)
  • Crew rate $/hr (display only) — fully-loaded rate for ordinary hours

⚙ Material rate overrides

  • Solar Mat $/kW — default $80, edit per quote if cabling unusual
  • Racking $/kW — default $60, edit if tile/tin/ground mount
  • Battery Mat $ — default $500 flat, edit if extra cabling/protection

⚙ Hours-per-component overrides

  • Hrs/string — default 1.5h per 7 panels. Bump for difficult roof access
  • Hrs/battery module — default 0.5h per module (+ 1h base)

⚙ Anchor day buttons (7.6 / 10 / 12 / 14)

Sets PLC_ANCHOR — the day length used for OT thresholds, fixed-cost day rounding, and travel hours. Default 12h = max-OT-day before resetting. Switch to:

  • 7.6h — strict 8-hour day (no OT, day flips at end of ordinary)
  • 10h — short OT day (caps OT at 2.4 hrs)
  • 12h — default, allows full 4.4 hrs OT before next day starts
  • 14h — long-day mode (rare; allows full OT bracket)

⚙ Fixed cost daily rates

  • Overheads $/day — default $800. Annual overheads ÷ working days/yr
  • Other Wages $/day — default $800. Office/sales staff annual ÷ working days

Both are non-GST (already net). Edit when business overhead structure changes — e.g. new vehicle, larger office.

⚙ Sale price & reverse calc

  • Sale Price ex GST — type a quote price; profit, margin, ★ recalc live
  • Target ★ + Set — type a target rating, click Set, sale price back-fills to hit it
  • Margin % (display) — net profit ÷ sale price
  • PPH (display) — profit per crew hour
  • ★ rating (display) — PPH ÷ 100, the headline quality metric

⚙ Output rows (read-only)

  • Materials Total · Overheads (with day mult) · Sale Coms · Battery Materials · Solar Materials · Racking
  • Sum — all costs except labour/wages/rebates
  • Labour · Other Wages · Battery Rebate · STC Rebate
  • Gross Profit — sale − materials
  • Net Profit — sale − everything (the headline)

10. Workflow Tips

  • Customer present? Toggle off P&L Calc, set Brand to single (not Compare), expand Solar & Battery Tools only, hand the phone over.
  • Oddball job? Skip Price Map, build line items in P&L Calc, set target ★, click Set.
  • Comparing GW vs Sigen? Brand → All · Compare. Cards render side-by-side.
  • Pricing changed? Edit in Price Table, both Map and P&L update instantly.
  • New version? Just close and re-open the pinned tile — no-cache fetches fresh every load.
